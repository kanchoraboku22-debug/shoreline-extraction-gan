# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all, regardless of gender, sexual orientation, disability, ethnicity, religion, or other identity aspects. This Code of Conduct outlines our expectations for all those who participate in our community, as well as the consequences for unacceptable behavior.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

- **Respectful Communication** - Use welcoming and inclusive language
- **Constructive Feedback** - Offer and accept criticism gracefully
- **Collaborative Approach** - Work together toward common goals
- **Inclusivity** - Welcome diverse perspectives and backgrounds
- **Professional Conduct** - Maintain a professional tone
- **Acknowledgment** - Give credit to others' contributions
- **Focus on Science** - Center discussions on data and evidence

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, discrimination, or exclusionary language
- Personal attacks or insults
- Disruptive or trolling behavior
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Publishing others' private information without consent
- Other conduct that violates professional standards

## Enforcement

### Reporting Violations

If you witness or experience Code of Conduct violations:

1. **Document the incident** - Note date, time, people involved, context
2. **Report privately** - Email maintainers at [email] with details
3. **Confidentiality** - Your report will be handled confidentially
4. **No retaliation** - We prohibit retaliation against reporters

### Investigation and Resolution

1. **Prompt review** - We will investigate promptly and thoroughly
2. **Fair process** - Both parties will have opportunity to respond
3. **Proportionate action** - Response matches severity of violation
4. **Transparency** - We will communicate decisions

### Possible Outcomes

- **Warning** - Clear expectation for future conduct
- **Temporary suspension** - From participation in project
- **Permanent removal** - From community and repositories

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:
- GitHub issues and pull requests
- Email communications
- Project meetings and discussions
- Official events representing the project
- All project spaces

## Expected Conduct

### For All Participants

- Treat everyone with respect and dignity
- Assume good intent, address concerns professionally
- Focus on the issue, not the person
- Accept responsibility for mistakes
- Help maintain a safe, inclusive environment

### For Project Maintainers

- Model the conduct we expect from others
- Enforce this Code of Conduct consistently and fairly
- Provide clear guidance and constructive feedback
- Support community members in meeting standards
- Lead by example

## Full Code of Conduct Details

### Diversity and Inclusion

We celebrate diversity and welcome contributions from people of all backgrounds. We believe that diverse perspectives strengthen our work and our community.

We commit to:
- Creating space for underrepresented voices
- Actively addressing bias and discrimination
- Providing accommodations for people with disabilities
- Welcoming and supporting new contributors

### Collaborative Community

We thrive on collaboration and mutual respect. We expect community members to:

- Help each other succeed
- Share knowledge and expertise generously
- Review others' work constructively
- Celebrate successes together
- Support colleagues facing challenges

### Professional Standards

All interactions should maintain professional standards:

- Use language appropriate for a scientific community
- Focus on technical issues, not personal opinions
- Provide evidence-based arguments
- Acknowledge uncertainty and limitations
- Admit mistakes gracefully

### Specific Expectations

#### In Code Reviews

- Provide constructive criticism focused on code, not character
- Offer suggestions for improvement
- Acknowledge good work
- Be patient with learning and mistakes
- Help others improve their skills

#### In Issue Discussions

- Stay on topic
- Provide clear, complete information
- Follow up on questions about your code
- Respect others' time and effort
- Avoid heated or personal arguments

#### In Communication Channels

- Use clear, respectful language
- Avoid harassment or discrimination
- Respect privacy and confidentiality
- Not spam or off-topic content
- Welcome diverse perspectives

### Accessibility

We strive to make our community accessible to everyone:

- Provide alt text for images
- Use clear language and structure
- Respond to accessibility concerns quickly
- Offer accommodations when needed
- Continuously improve accessibility
